## The Seven Principles

### 1. The Principle of Mentalism

**"THE ALL is MIND; The Universe is Mental."** — The Kybalion

**Applied to Tesseract:**

Your mind is sacred space. What enters it shapes your reality. The Tesseract respects this by:

- **Filtering noise** - Only relevant information passes through
- **Preserving attention** - Your focus is precious, not unlimited
- **Enabling choice** - You decide what to adopt, when

The system doesn't flood you with everything. It shows you what matters *to you*, when you're ready to receive it.

**Try this:** Notice how the Tesseract asks "Is this relevant to your workflow?" before showing you updates. That's Mentalism—respecting your mental space.

---

### 2. The Principle of Correspondence

**"As above, so below; as below, so above."** — The Kybalion

**Applied to Tesseract:**

Patterns at one level mirror patterns at other levels. When you learn a principle, you gain insight across domains.

The Tesseract emphasizes:

- **Patterns over recipes** - Learn why, not just how
- **Transferable knowledge** - Concepts that apply broadly
- **Deep structure** - The mechanics beneath the surface

A workflow pattern you discover in Claude Code might apply to your testing strategy, your deployment process, your learning approach. The principle is what matters.

**Watch for:** When learning a new feature, ask "What's the underlying pattern here?" That's Correspondence—seeing the universal in the specific.

---

### 3. The Principle of Vibration

**"Nothing rests; everything moves; everything vibrates."** — The Kybalion

**Applied to Tesseract:**

Everything in tech is constantly evolving. Claude Code today isn't Claude Code tomorrow. You must stay current without chasing every trend.

The Tesseract handles this by:

- **Tracking velocity** - How fast is something changing?
- **Identifying stability** - Which patterns are enduring?
- **Timing adoption** - When to adopt vs. when to wait

Some updates are high-frequency noise. Others are fundamental shifts. The system helps you tell the difference.

**This works because:** Not all vibrations deserve your attention. The Tesseract filters for signal, not just frequency.

---

### 4. The Principle of Polarity

**"Everything is Dual; everything has poles."** — The Kybalion

**Applied to Tesseract:**

Every technology choice has trade-offs. No solution is purely good or bad.

The Tesseract presents:

- **Honest trade-offs** - Pros AND cons
- **Context sensitivity** - Good for X, not for Y
- **Balanced judgment** - Multiple perspectives

We don't hype. We don't FUD. We show the full picture and help you decide based on *your* context.

**Field note:** When the system recommends something, it also explains what you're giving up. That's Polarity—acknowledging both poles of every choice.

---

### 5. The Principle of Rhythm

**"Everything flows, out and in; everything has its tides."** — The Kybalion

**Applied to Tesseract:**

Learning has natural rhythms. You can't force understanding—it emerges in its own time.

The Tesseract respects this by:

- **Pacing delivery** - Not everything at once
- **Honoring readiness** - Learn when you're ready
- **Building gradually** - Foundation before advanced

Some days you're ready to learn. Other days you're building. The system adapts to your rhythm, not the other way around.

**Try this:** Use the "Conservative" sync strategy at first. Let updates accumulate. Review when YOU'RE ready. That's Rhythm—honoring your natural pace.

---

### 6. The Principle of Cause and Effect

**"Every Cause has its Effect; every Effect has its Cause."** — The Kybalion

**Applied to Tesseract:**

The system tracks not just *what* you adopt, but *what happens when you do*.

This enables:

- **Impact assessment** - Did this actually help?
- **Causal learning** - What led to what?
- **Pattern recognition** - Which changes produce which results?

You're not guessing. You have data. You know cause and effect in *your* environment.

**Watch for:** The adoption log asking "What was the impact?" That's Cause and Effect—learning from consequences.

---

### 7. The Principle of Gender

**"Gender is in everything; everything has its Masculine and Feminine Principles."** — The Kybalion

**Applied to Tesseract:**

Knowledge creation requires both:

**Masculine** (Active/Projective):
- Seeking new information
- Testing hypotheses
- Building and shipping
- Taking action

**Feminine** (Receptive/Integrative):
- Absorbing insights
- Connecting patterns
- Reflecting on results
- Allowing understanding to emerge

The Tesseract balances both:

**Fetch & Apply** (Masculine) — Active acquisition and implementation
**Curate & Track** (Feminine) — Thoughtful filtering and integration

Both are necessary. Pure action without reflection is chaos. Pure contemplation without action is stagnation.

**This works because:** Real mastery requires both doing and understanding, acting and integrating.

## Practical Application

### When reviewing updates:

Ask yourself:
- **Mentalism:** Is this worth mental real estate?
- **Correspondence:** What's the underlying pattern?
- **Vibration:** Is this a fundamental shift or noise?
- **Polarity:** What am I trading off?
- **Rhythm:** Am I ready to learn this now?
- **Cause & Effect:** What results do I expect?
- **Gender:** Do I need to act, or reflect first?

### When sharing knowledge:

Remember:
- **Mentalism** - Respect others' attention
- **Correspondence** - Share the pattern, not just the recipe
- **Vibration** - Distinguish stable from changing
- **Polarity** - Show trade-offs honestly
- **Rhythm** - Let others learn at their pace
- **Cause & Effect** - Share results, not just methods
- **Gender** - Balance showing and guiding
